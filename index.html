Separating Constraint Compliance
from Semantic Accuracy

A benchmark for evaluating instruction-following robustness under prompt compression. CDCT independently measures constraint compliance (CC) and semantic accuracy (SA) across five compression levels, revealing a universal U-shaped failure pattern and establishing its causal mechanism via a controlled helpfulness ablation.

Results

Principal findings

Across 88 experimental conditions (11 models × 8 concepts × 5 compression levels), evaluated by a three-judge LLM jury (Qwen3-32B, GLM-5, Nemotron-Super-3-120B), five results stand out.

01 / UNIVERSAL

Universal U-shaped CC degradation

87/88 conditions (98.9%) exhibit a U-shaped constraint compliance curve. CC is high at extreme compression (c=0.0: 0.935) and full context (c=1.0: 0.931), but collapses at medium compression (c=0.5: 0.178). The pattern holds across all architectures and all eight concept domains.

Magnitude = 0.754
02 / SURPRISING

SA is not monotone — it also dips at c=0.5

Prior characterizations of SA as monotonically increasing with context are incorrect. Mean SA at c=0.5 (0.771) is significantly lower than at both extremes (0.883–0.934), indicating a shared instruction ambiguity zone that simultaneously degrades behavioral compliance and semantic quality.

SA at c=0.5 = 0.771
03 / CAUSAL

RLHF helpfulness is the shared causal driver

Removing helpfulness alignment signals from system prompts at c=0.5 recovers CC from 0.178 to 0.977 (+447.8%) and SA from 0.771 to 0.987 (+21.5 pp) simultaneously across all 88 trials. The joint recovery under a single intervention establishes a shared causal mechanism rather than independent degradation.

p = 6.26 × 10⁻⁷³
04 / SPECIFIC

Ablation effect is compression-specific

The helpfulness ablation reduces CC at c=0.0 (Δ=−0.184) and c=1.0 (Δ=−0.277) while producing a large gain only at c=0.5 (Δ=+0.799). This specificity rules out a uniform trade-off and supports the interpretation that helpfulness signals are necessary at full context but pathological at medium compression.

Δ at c=0.5 = +0.799
05 / ARCHITECTURE

Extended reasoning partially mitigates trough depth

Grok-4 (grok-4-20-reasoning) achieves CC=0.375 at c=0.5, 107% above the efficient model median (0.181). This is consistent with the hypothesis that chain-of-thought reasoning provides structural scaffolding that reduces sensitivity to instruction ambiguity, though the improvement is partial and the U-curve remains.

Grok-4 CC@0.5 = 0.375
06 / PRACTICAL

Extreme compression dominates medium compression

A 2-word prompt yields higher CC (0.935) and higher SA (0.883) than a 27-word prompt (CC=0.178, SA=0.771). For applications where the target output is well-defined, minimal prompting is demonstrably safer than partial specification.

CC@c=0.0 = 0.935

Methodology

Experimental design

CDCT disentangles two failure modes that prior instruction-following benchmarks conflate: constraint violation (a behavioral failure) and semantic degradation (a knowledge failure). Each is measured independently across five compression levels.

01

Concept selection

Eight concepts across three domains (formal sciences, natural sciences, applied sciences), selected for unambiguous ground truth and resistance to paraphrase. All are sufficiently narrow to evaluate precise explanation in 35 words.

Modus PonensRecursionDerivative Natural SelectionF=ma Harm PrincipleImpressionismPhoneme
02

Compression protocol

Five levels (c ∈ {0.0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0}) ranging from extreme compression (~2 words) to no compression (~135 words). The word-count constraint (exactly 35 words) is held constant across all levels; only context availability varies. Prompts are generated via model-based rewriting to preserve semantic coherence.

c=0.0 (~2w)c=0.25 (~13w)c=0.5 (~27w)c=0.75 (~69w)c=1.0 (~135w)
03

Three-judge LLM jury

Each response is independently scored by three architecturally diverse judge models on Constraint Compliance (CC, 0–1), Semantic Accuracy (SA, 0–1), and Functional Completeness (FC, 0–1). Scores are averaged across judges. Inter-judge agreement is computed per compression level and reported as a calibration metric.

Qwen3-32BGLM-5Nemotron-Super-3-120B
04

Helpfulness ablation

All 88 conditions were re-evaluated at c=0.5 under a no_helpfulness system prompt that removes elaboration-encouraging RLHF alignment language while preserving the word-count constraint. Paired t-tests and Cohen's d are reported. The ablation is repeated identically across all models and concepts; no per-model tuning is applied.
